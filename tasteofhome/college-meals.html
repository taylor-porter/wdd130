Easy Meals to make in college

Written by Taylor Porter - 11/21/2024

College is stressful! Moving away from home and meeting new people while keeping up with your school work is a lot! Sometimes you just want something simple and comforting. These recipes are super easy to put together, and can be a saving grace for stressed students.

Ramen

A nice bowl of ramen is a college classic, and can be a great comfort food if you're short on time or ingredients. I love this recipe from Budget Bytes for spicy peanut butter ramen noodles. I know. Peanut butter in ramen!? Trust me, it’s worth a try. The peanut butter helps create a delicious creamy sauce and adds a bit of sustenance and protein to the meal. I would recommend using a bit under ¼ cup in the sauce to have a creamer sauce. I also substituted chili garlic sauce with hot sauce with garlic powder and chili flakes for a while until I found the real deal at the grocery store. Personally, I like to add an egg and some frozen peas to the ramen while it’s cooking and top it with some sliced ham, but like the recipe says, feel free to add what you want!

Ingredients
  • 2 tablespoons of peanut butter
  • 1 tablespoon of Chili Garlic sauce (I usually use hot sauce with a pinch of chilli flakes and garlic powder)
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 2 teaspoons of brown sugar ( or 1 tablespoon if using natural peanut butter)
  • 1 packet of ramen
  • An egg, peas, 2-3 slices of ham (cut into pieces), or whatever you like!
  • Cut-up chives as a garnish (optional)
Directions
  1. In a small pot, start boiling water.
  2. Put the peanut butter, hot sauce, soy sauce, and brown sugar into a small bowl. Then, mix the ingredients together to create a paste.
  3. Add a ¼ cup of water to the paste to make the sauce
  4. Once the water is boiling, cook the ramen (3 mins). You can put an egg in, and add peas at about 30 seconds to go
  5. Drain the ramen and add the ham, or any toppings you would like.
  6. Add the sauce and stir the ramen.
  7. Enjoy!

Serves 1-2

Chicken Curry With Rice

Curry and rice has always been one of my go-to meals at home. Luckily, you can get curry sauce from the grocery store, making it super easy to make. If you have some canned chicken around, it’s even easier! Curry is one of my favorite ways to eat canned chicken and make it come to life. Simply put the chicken in a pan with a bit of oil, and add the curry sauce and let it simmer. Add some rice and vegetables, and you’ve got an effortless, delicious meal!

Ingredients
  • A can of curry sauce of your choice
  • Chicken
  • Olive / vegetable oil
  • Rice

You can also use vegtables, shrimp, or another protein with or instead of the chicken.

Directions

The can should come with directions, and they usually go something like this:

  1. Cut the chicken into cubes
  2. Cook the chicken in a pan with a little oil for 5 munutes, or until golden brown
  3. Add the sauce and let it simmer for about 15 minutes or until the chicken is cooked through (165° F / 73.8 ° C)

Barbecue Chicken Quesadilla

I love myself a dang quesadilla! It’s definitely a life-saver when I’m in a crunch. Simply put a tortilla on a pan, add cheese, and boom! I always add a bit of extra cheese spilling out of the sides and on top of the quesadilla to get some nice, crispy cheese. It’s probably not good for cholesterol, but it’s good for my soul. I had some leftover grilled chicken one night, and I decided to chop it up and add it to my quesadilla with a spiral of barbeque sauce to give my quesadilla some extra protein and flavor.

Ingredients
  • Tortilla
  • Shredded Cheese
  • Barbecue sauce
  • Grilled chicken (this is a great way to use leftovers)
Directions
  1. Add the tortilla to a pan.
  2. Sprinkle with a ton of cheese
  3. Cut the chicken up into chunks, and sprinkle them on.
  4. Add a drizzle of barbecue sauce
  5. You can fold it in half, or add another tortilla on top
  6. Add cheese on top and on the side for crispy cheese
  7. Cook the quesadilla. When You are able to lift the cheese of the side from the pan, flip the quesadilla over/
  8. Once the cheese on the bottom is golden brown, take the quesadilla of the pan. Slice and enjoy!

Salsa Chicken

This is the perfect set-it-and-forget-it dish. This dish cooks nicely in a slow, but you can also put it in the oven. The creamy, tender chicken only requires 3 ingredients - chicken breasts, salsa, and cream cheese.

Ingredients
  • 3-4 chicken breasts
  • 1 package of cream cheese
  • 1-2 cups of salsa
  • Grilled chicken (this is a great way to use leftovers)
Directions
  1. Put the 3-4 chicken breasts in the slow cooker or a baking dish
  2. Add a package of cream cheese and smother the chicken in the salsa
  3. Cook in the slow cooker on low for 6-8 hours, on high for 3-4 hours, or cook it in the oven at 350° for 40ish minutes
  4. Shred the chicken while stirring everything together, and serve over rice
